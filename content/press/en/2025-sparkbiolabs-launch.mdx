SparkBioLabs (CEO Hongju Lee; formerly SparkBio), part of SparkLabs Group, announced on December 11 that it has officially launched as an accelerator for bio startups and is beginning full program operations.

On December 10, SparkBioLabs received its AC registration certificate from Minister of SMEs and Startups Sung-sook Han at the "2025 Startup Entrepreneur Growth Summit" event of COMEUP 2025 held at COEX in Seoul, completing its official registration as a specialized bio accelerator.

In a departure from the uniform, cohort-centric support model historically used by accelerators, SparkBioLabs has adopted a rolling-admissions system with no separate recruitment windows. Each applicant company will be evaluated individually and provided with programs optimized for its stage-specific growth needs and its technology's particular characteristics.

SparkBioLabs creates an AC program environment designed to quickly address the core challenges facing bio startups — including proof-of-concept (PoC), clinical trials, regulatory response, and business expansion strategy. It will also actively support global market entry and investment linkage by leveraging SparkLabs Group's networks across the United States, Singapore, Saudi Arabia, Taiwan, and Australia.

SparkLabs Group has built bio infrastructure through initiatives such as establishing a specialized clinical trial hospital, and currently supports 11 bio and wellness startups — including Sysmex Korea, PeopleBio, and Waycen.

Hongju Lee, CEO of SparkBioLabs, said, "Bio startups face many hurdles from early growth through scaling, so the key is selecting the support they need most at each business stage. By combining tailored support with SparkLabs Group's broader acceleration know-how, we will contribute to revitalizing Korea's bio-health startup ecosystem."

SparkBioLabs will create a dedicated AC program application section on its official website to support ongoing applications. SparkLabs Group also plans to continue expanding its specialized acceleration capabilities across AI, deep tech, and bio — actively supporting the global market entry of outstanding technology startups.
