Global accelerator SparkLabs (CEO Eugene Kim) announced that it has produced top-ranked startups at the K-Startup Grand Challenge (KSGC) for two consecutive years, extending a track record that goes beyond acceleration to validated outcomes.

Now in its 11th year, the K-Startup Grand Challenge is the Ministry of SMEs and Startups' flagship global entrepreneurship program, supporting outstanding foreign founding teams in establishing Korean entities and settling into the local ecosystem. Selected startups receive commercialization funding plus approximately nine months of acceleration, networking, and other support.

As a specialist accelerator for KSGC since 2024, SparkLabs demonstrated its development capabilities from its very first year — Polymerize, an AI-powered R&D solution for new materials, received the program's grand prize.

Among the 2025 cohort, AI-based language learning platform AvoLabs took first place. Other SparkLabs-supported companies — Albarabs, a blockchain/edge-AI verification platform for aerospace and critical infrastructure, and Bioon, a developer of portable food allergy diagnostic kits — also placed in the top rankings.

SparkLabs runs the program well beyond pure acceleration: high-potential companies receive follow-on investment as well. Three companies — including Piero Company, an operator of IT device subscription and redistribution services — have already received SparkLabs' support to settle into the Korean market.

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, said, "K-Startup Grand Challenge is a program that provides real, practical support for foreign startups entering and establishing themselves in the Korean market. For companies we invest in through the program, we will continue to provide global network access and follow-on support."

Separately, since 2012 SparkLabs has operated a batch program and made follow-on investments in early-stage Korean startups — growing and adding more than 320 companies to its portfolio, including Wanted Lab, Memebox, QuadMedicine, Sentbe, Caredoc, and Muselive.
