Global accelerator SparkLabs (CEO Eugene Kim) announced a seed investment in TeamKy Inc. (CEO Doah Kim), a provider and operator of customer-service AI agents. Murex Partners also participated in the round.

TeamKy was founded in 2024 by Doah Kim, former COO of Korea's leading travel platform MyRealTrip. Over ten years of overseeing customer-service operations in the high-complexity travel domain — covering airlines, accommodations, and tours — Kim built the operational expertise and domain understanding that now anchor TeamKy.

TeamKy's core offering is AI agent technology that directly handles the full scope of agent work. Its services include adoption consulting; AI agent implementation through direct integration with internal systems; and full outsourcing of customer-service operations.

TeamKy has already built a diverse client base across travel, e-commerce, and consumer goods — growing rapidly enough to approach KRW 100 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) within just one year of founding.

SparkLabs decided on the investment based on its assessment that TeamKy's AI capabilities — including a task-completion rate of over 60% and a hallucination rate converging to 0% — will deliver competitive advantage in the global AI agent market. On SparkLabs' recommendation, TeamKy was also selected for TIPS (Tech Incubator Program for Startups), the Korean Ministry of SMEs and Startups' technology-based startup support program.

With the investment, TeamKy plans to pursue R&D for further technical improvements to increase its AI agent response rates and begin actively acquiring new clients.

Doah Kim, CEO of TeamKy, said, "The adoption of AI agents is enabling dramatic cost reduction and changing the paradigm of customer-service outsourcing. Drawing on ten years of experience, we will offer our clients an environment in which AI fully handles customer-service operations and position TeamKy as an AI innovation partner accountable for service outcomes."

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, added, "TeamKy is a team that genuinely understands the real-world problems of customer-service operations and has structured its solution around them. We expect them to transform the AI contact center from a cost-centered function into a core organization that simultaneously elevates customer experience and operational efficiency."
