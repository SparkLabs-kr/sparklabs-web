SparkLabs Partners (CEO Hoemin Kim), the venture capital arm of SparkLabs Group, announced that it has signed a four-way MOU with Handong University, Pohang TechnoPark, and the Korea Angel Investment Association to establish a specialized clinical trial hospital in Gyeongsangbuk-do Province.

The MOU is part of SparkBioLabs' ongoing effort to build a bio and medical innovation ecosystem within the "Pohang Global Enterprise Innovation Park," bringing together regional universities, research institutes, medical institutions, and private investors to build an integrated bio cluster spanning clinical research to technology commercialization.

Under the agreement, the four institutions will collaborate on the establishment and operation of a specialized clinical trial hospital; joint bio and medical research; clinical validation and commercialization support for bio startups; and the development of global research networks. The goal is to grow Pohang into a Northeast Asian bio innovation hub and to build a growth ladder for bio startups anchored in regional medical infrastructure.

Handong University will lead industry-academia-research initiatives in medical science and bioengineering; Pohang TechnoPark will handle regional enterprise support and technology commercialization; Pohang Semyung Christian Hospital will provide clinical trial execution and medical data-based research infrastructure; and SparkBioLabs will lead bio startup sourcing, investment, and global network linkage. With clearly defined roles across the four institutions, the partnership lays practical foundations for implementing a clinically-focused bio cluster model.

Hongju Lee, CEO of SparkBioLabs, said, "Through this agreement, we will build a full-cycle bio innovation platform spanning R&D, clinical trials, and commercialization. By combining regional medical infrastructure with SparkLabs Group's global network and centering on the establishment of a specialized clinical hospital, we will deliver tangible results that help grow Pohang into a global bio hub."
