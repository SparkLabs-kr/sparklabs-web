SparkLabs Partners (CEO Hoemin Kim), the venture capital arm of SparkLabs Group, announced that it is moving forward with the establishment of a specialized clinical trial hospital in the Pohang region.

To this end, SparkLabs Partners signed a four-way MOU on the "Establishment of a Specialized Clinical Hospital and Bio Cluster" with Handong University, Pohang Technopark, and the Korea Angel Investment Association at Handong University's Hyeondong Hall.

Under the agreement, the four institutions will cooperate on the establishment and operation of the specialized clinical trial hospital in Pohang, with shared goals of: concentrated development of clinically-focused bio startups; joint industry-academia-research medical science and bio research; strategic planning and activities to secure investment resources; provision of clinical trial and research infrastructure; and acceleration of validation and commercialization — all aimed at building an integrated bio cluster.

This project combines regional medical institutions, research bodies, and private investors in a practical partnership to develop Pohang as a bio innovation hub spanning clinical research to technology commercialization, while also establishing a growth ladder for bio startups anchored in regional medical infrastructure.

Hoemin Kim, CEO of SparkLabs Partners, said, "Until now, many companies have struggled to conduct clinical trials in Korea due to insufficient conditions, often incurring significant costs by pursuing trials abroad. Through the establishment of a specialized clinical hospital, we will meaningfully implement a clinically-focused bio cluster model and grow Pohang into a global bio hub."
