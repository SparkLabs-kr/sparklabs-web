SparkBioLabs (CEO Hongju Lee; formerly SparkBio), part of SparkLabs Group, announced that it has signed an MOU with IGIS Asset Management (Co-CEOs Younggoo Kang and Kyusung Lee) to establish and operate space infrastructure dedicated to life sciences companies.

The agreement combines SparkBioLabs' expertise in bio startup development and space operations with IGIS Asset Management's real estate development and operation capabilities — creating foundations for the sustainable growth of Korea's life sciences industry.

Under the agreement, the two firms will jointly identify and build facilities specialized for the life sciences industry, including research and experimental labs equipped with state-of-the-art equipment; cleanrooms dedicated to clinical trials and testing; and ultra-low-temperature logistics facilities for reagent and sample storage. These facilities will then be offered to bio startups at reasonable cost.

SparkBioLabs is a specialized accelerator established by SparkLabs Group to develop and support high-potential bio startups. It is rapidly expanding by securing bio startup and research infrastructure at key locations across Korea, including the Seoul metropolitan area.

Hongju Lee, CEO of SparkBioLabs, said, "Growth in the bio industry requires not only research and investment, but also advanced spatial infrastructure. Through this partnership with IGIS Asset Management, we will help Korea's bio startups grow in research and business environments of global standard."

Gwanshik Yoon, Managing Director of the Strategic Investment Group at IGIS Asset Management, said, "SparkBioLabs is a specialized institution that has worked closely with both domestic and global bio ecosystems. We expect this agreement to create a new point of connection between real estate and the bio industry, delivering long-term synergy in life sciences — a sector spotlighted as a future engine of national growth."
