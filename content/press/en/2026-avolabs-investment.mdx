Global accelerator SparkLabs (CEO Eugene Kim) announced its investment in AvoLabs (CEO Deniz Albayrak), the operator of AI-powered English learning solution "Avolingo."

Founded by Turkish-American entrepreneur Deniz Albayrak, AvoLabs leverages AI technology to reduce teachers' workload and improve classroom efficiency. Powered by proprietary algorithms and automation technology, it supports the full range of digital classroom operations.

The company's flagship solution, Avolingo, generates customized assignment content based on the materials and curricula uploaded by teachers — optimized for each student's learning level and goals — and automatically handles grading and feedback.

Through an integrated dashboard, the platform also provides at-a-glance data on student learning progress, including real-time progress monitoring, achievement analysis, and growth report generation.

Avolingo already supplies its solution to educational institutions in Korea, Turkey, Germany, and Malaysia, proving its global scalability. In March, it was selected as the top-ranked startup at the Phase-3 Performance Sharing Session of the "K-Startup Grand Challenge (KSGC) 2025" program, hosted by the Ministry of SMEs and Startups and the Korea Institute of Startup & Entrepreneurship Development.

With this investment, AvoLabs plans to expand its solution to Asian markets with strong demand for English education and enhance the platform to support a wider range of languages.

Deniz Albayrak, CEO of AvoLabs, commented, "Through SparkLabs' investment, we will strengthen our foothold in Korea and accelerate our global expansion. We hope Avolingo transforms textbook-centric education into 'living English learning' that teachers and students build together."

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, said, "AvoLabs is a team with the competitive edge to improve learning operations through AI while preserving existing systems. With its globally validated scalability, we expect them to define a new standard in education."

Separately, since 2024 SparkLabs has operated the K-Startup Grand Challenge program, selecting high-potential companies such as Polymerize, Piero Company, and AvoLabs and providing follow-on investment. Through recent inbound and outbound programs with the Middle East, SparkLabs is also expanding its role as a global bridge — supporting foreign startups entering Korea and Korean companies expanding overseas.
