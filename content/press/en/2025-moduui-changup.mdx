Global accelerator SparkLabs (CEO Eugene Kim) has been selected as an operating agency for the General and Tech Track of "Everyone's Startup Project," the large-scale startup audition program hosted by Korea's Ministry of SMEs and Startups.

"Everyone's Startup Project" is a government-backed startup support program that directly invests in and develops founders in stages, enabling any citizen to take on startup challenges with only an idea. The current integrated recruitment will select 5,000 startup challengers in total — 4,000 in the General and Tech tracks and 1,000 in the Local track. SparkLabs is one of approximately nine Seoul-region accelerators named as a General and Tech track operator.

The General and Tech Track is open to aspiring founders and companies within three years of incorporation who are pursuing creative ideas or technology-based startups. Applicants go through a four-stage audition process: idea screening (4,000 applicants); regional preliminaries (500); regional open IR (200); region-wide closed IR (100); and the national final audition. Throughout the process, participants receive phased support including startup activity funds, MVP production funds, mentoring, and commercialization funding. Final winners receive up to KRW 1 billion in support, including connections to investment.

What distinguishes this program is that it is not a simple prize-based audition but a structured startup development model spanning idea discovery, prototype development, IR pitching, and investment linkage. Even at the earliest idea stage, participants can secure the opportunity to be validated by real investors.

SparkLabs has operated as a private TIPS (Tech Incubator Program for Startups) operator for ten years, selecting 63 companies and running a 15-week batch program linked to seed investment. To date, the firm has invested in approximately 320 startups across 24 cohorts, accumulating deep experience in supporting early-stage startups. SparkLabs is particularly differentiated in its scaleup capabilities for technology-based founding teams — from growth strategy development and IR preparation to investment linkage — extending from early stages through investment validation.

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, said, "Because Everyone's Startup is structured around the government directly investing in founders, even early-stage ideas are worth taking on if the market problem and execution viability are clearly defined. Drawing on our nearly decade of startup development experience, we will focus on helping founders shape their businesses to the point where their ideas can pass real investment validation."

General and Tech Track recruitment for Everyone's Startup runs through May 15. Applicants can apply online via the official Everyone's Startup platform, and offline information sessions will be held sequentially across 16 regions nationwide.
