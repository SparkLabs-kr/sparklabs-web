The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT) of Saudi Arabia, through its Digital Entrepreneurship Center (CODE), successfully concluded the "Tech Founders in Korea 2025" program, which was operated by SparkLabs Saudi Arabia in Seoul as part of the international incubation track of MCIT's Tech Founders initiative.

The program hosted 20 Saudi tech startups, selected by the Saudi government, for a six-week immersion in Korea from November 3 to December 12, where founders explored Korea's innovation ecosystem, engaged with leading universities, and developed potential collaboration and market-entry opportunities.

The closing ceremony, held on December 12 at the Grand Ballroom of COEX, brought together high-level representatives from Saudi government entities and partner institutions, including:

- MCIT Deputy Minister Eng. Mohammed AlRobayan
- Assistant Deputy Minister Eng. Mohammed Alariefy
- CODE General Manager Eng. Abdullah Alshamrani
- NTDP CEO Eng. Ibrahim Neyaz

Representatives from top Korean universities — including Seoul National University and Korea University — as well as officials from Korean ministries and major corporations such as SPC and NAVER, were also in attendance.

During the event, Deputy Minister Eng. Mohammed AlRobayan and SparkLabs Co-CEO Han-Joo Lee delivered keynote remarks, highlighting the program's role in strengthening ties between Saudi Arabia and Korea's innovation ecosystems.

The ceremony showcased key program outcomes — including 10+ MOUs, corporate collaborations, and several partnership frameworks built between Saudi startups and Korean institutions — underscoring that the initiative extended far beyond cultural exchange to establish practical, actionable, and sustainable cooperation between both countries.

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, commented, "Through Tech Founders, our collaboration with the Saudi government and partner agencies has grown even stronger. We remain committed to serving as a global bridge that supports the growth of Saudi startups and the international expansion of Korean innovators."

SparkLabs continues to support Korean companies exploring Middle Eastern opportunities. Earlier this year, the accelerator facilitated a delegation of 29 Korean SMEs to key regional markets, including visits to Rebellions, Riiid Technologies, and DoubleMe — reflecting the rising interest in Saudi Arabia as a global innovation destination.
