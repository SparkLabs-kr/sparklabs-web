Global accelerator SparkLabs (CEO Eugene Kim) announced its investment in Piero Company Inc. (CEO Minjin Park), an operator of IT device subscription and redistribution services. CNT Tech co-invested in this round.

Piero Company manages a fleet of more than 10,000 IT devices through its subscription service "Phone-Go" and its collection and redistribution service "Re-Go," offering reasonably priced rentals while helping client companies monetize idle or abandoned IT assets through acquisition and settlement services. The company currently serves a diverse customer base ranging from major Korean enterprises to startups.

A distinctive strength of Piero Company is "Re-Go Trade" — a program that converts vetted recovered IT devices into tradable assets and redistributes them to buyers in emerging and developing markets. Through Re-Go Trade, the company has built a global redistribution channel.

With this investment, Piero Company plans to advance its trade infrastructure into a platform-based offering and attract new domestic clients looking to dispose of idle IT assets. The company will also work closely with SparkLabs to accelerate its international expansion.

Eugene Kim, CEO of SparkLabs, said, "As the realization of a circular economy has become a national priority, we see Piero Company — which finds value-creating use cases for IT devices across their full lifecycle — as well-positioned to compete in the global redistribution market. That view drove our investment decision."

Minjin Park, CEO of Piero Company, said, "Our goal is to build a new system in which IT device assets circulate sustainably around the world. We will expand Re-Go Trade in phases, turning it into a new growth engine that delivers global impact."

Piero Company previously received SparkLabs' acceleration through the 2024 K-Startup Grand Challenge program, which supports Asian market entry, and was also selected for the Ministry of SMEs and Startups' TIPS program. The company recently signed an IPO underwriting agreement with Korea Investment & Securities, laying the groundwork for a public offering.
